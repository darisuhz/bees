Colonies


Bee colonies are groups of related insects that organize themselves cooperatively.
Although they are individually independent, they are interdependent and cannot
survive without the cooperation of their colony mates.

Queen Bees


Queen bees are tasked with laying eggs that will give rise to the new generation of
bees. Normally, a hive has only one queen bee. If it dies, the workers create a new
queen bee by feeding one of them a special diet called royal jelly.

Drones and Workers


Drones, which are male bees, do not work. Their main function is to feed on honey
and wait for the opportunity to mate.

Worker bees are the hardest working in the colony. They are responsible for
collecting nectar from flowers and transforming it into honey.

Pollination


Pollination plays a fundamental role in the production of seeds and fruits in flowering
plants. It is an essential process for the maintenance of life on Earth. Bees are highly
effective pollinating insects.
